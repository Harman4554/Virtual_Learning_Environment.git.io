Core Features of a VLE:

Course Content: A VLE gathers all course materials, including lessons, assignments, and quizzes.

Communication: It serves as a place for instructors and learners to meet virtually.

Feedback and Grading: Instructors can provide feedback and grade assignments within the VLE.

Student-Teacher Interaction: A VLE facilitates communication between students and educators.

Access Anytime, Anywhere: Learners can access VLEs from computers or mobile devices.

Benefits of Using a VLE:

Automation: VLEs automate daily tasks, saving time and money.

Productivity: Assemble e-learning materials, tools, and communications in one place.

Process Acceleration: Speed up tasks like grading and feedback.

Scalability: VLEs work for schools, colleges, and universities.

Improved Student Retention: Enhance engagement and personalized learning.

Examples of VLEs:

Canvas LMS: An open-source VLE that connects teachers and students.

Firefly: Allows teachers to link students to online resources and embed multimedia.

Integrated VLEs: Some platforms integrate additional functions like videoconferencing and collaboration
